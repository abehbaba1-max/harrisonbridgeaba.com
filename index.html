What We Offer

ABA therapy that meets your child where they are

Every service is delivered in your child's natural environment by compassionate, BCBA-supervised clinicians and trained RBTs.

In-Home

In-Home ABA Therapy

Our trained RBTs and BCBA supervisors bring individualized, naturalistic ABA directly into your home. Sessions target communication, daily living skills, social behaviors, and behavior reduction in the environment your child knows and trusts. Every plan is built around your family's schedule and your child's unique goals.

School-Based

School-Based Support

Our clinicians collaborate with teachers, aides, and IEP teams to carry ABA strategies into the classroom. We provide direct therapy support, data collection, and staff consultation — ensuring the skills your child learns at home show up at school every day.

Community

Community-Based Services

True independence means navigating the real world. We work with children in grocery stores, parks, libraries, and recreational centers — building the skills that matter most outside the home and therapy room.

Daycare

Daycare Consultation

We partner with daycare centers and early learning programs to provide on-site consultation, behavior support plans, and staff training — creating a consistent, supportive environment throughout your child's entire day.

Assessment

Behavioral Assessments

Before treatment begins, our BCBAs conduct comprehensive functional behavior assessments and skills evaluations. This forms the scientific foundation of every individualized treatment plan we build — identifying your child's strengths, priorities, and the functions behind challenging behaviors.

Family

Caregiver Training

Parents are the most important people in a child's life. Our structured coaching gives caregivers the strategies, tools, and confidence to support their child's growth 24/7 — because progress in therapy only sticks when it carries into everyday life.

FAQ

Answers to your most common questions

Everything families ask before getting started — answered honestly and clearly.

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a scientific, evidence-based approach to understanding and changing behavior. It is widely recognized as the gold standard treatment for Autism Spectrum Disorder. ABA uses data, positive reinforcement, and individualized treatment plans to help children build communication skills, daily living skills, social skills, and reduce challenging behaviors.

Harrison Bridge ABA primarily serves children ages 2 through 12. However, there is no hard cutoff — we evaluate each child individually. Contact us to discuss your child's specific situation and we will work with you to find the right fit.

Most Medicaid plans and many private insurance plans cover ABA therapy when there is a qualifying diagnosis such as Autism Spectrum Disorder and a physician recommendation. We verify your specific benefits at absolutely no cost to you. Contact us and we will check your coverage and walk you through your options clearly — no confusion, no surprises.

We come to you. Unlike center-based providers, Harrison Bridge ABA delivers every service in your child's natural environments — home, school, daycare, and community. Skills are learned where they matter most and generalize faster. We are also deeply committed to family partnership — parents are trained and involved in every step of the process.

Every session is tailored to your child's individualized treatment plan. Sessions involve structured activities, naturalistic teaching moments, data collection, and positive reinforcement. Our RBTs make sessions engaging and fun for children. Parent involvement is encouraged — we want caregivers to observe and participate so strategies carry over throughout the entire day.

After your initial consultation, we verify your insurance benefits typically within a few business days. We then schedule the BCBA assessment in your home. Once complete, we develop your child's treatment plan and therapy begins. The full process from first contact to therapy starting typically takes 2–4 weeks depending on insurance authorization timelines.

Yes. While ABA therapy is most commonly associated with Autism Spectrum Disorder, it is effective for children with developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, ADHD, and other behavioral challenges. Contact us to discuss your child's specific situation and we will assess the right fit.

A BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst) is a licensed clinician who has completed advanced graduate-level education, supervised clinical experience, and passed a national certification exam. They design and oversee all treatment plans. An RBT (Registered Behavior Technician) is a trained therapist who delivers the direct therapy sessions under BCBA supervision. At Harrison Bridge ABA, every plan is designed by a BCBA and delivered by a trained RBT.

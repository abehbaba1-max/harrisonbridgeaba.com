FAQ

Answers to your most common questions

Everything families ask before getting started — answered honestly and clearly.

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a scientific, evidence-based approach to understanding and changing behavior. It is widely recognized as the gold standard treatment for Autism Spectrum Disorder. ABA uses data, positive reinforcement, and individualized treatment plans to help children build communication skills, daily living skills, social skills, and reduce challenging behaviors.

Harrison Bridge ABA primarily serves children ages 2 through 12. However, we evaluate each child individually — there is no hard cutoff. Contact us to discuss your child's specific situation and we will work with you to find the right fit.

Most Medicaid plans and many private insurance plans cover ABA therapy when there is a qualifying diagnosis and a physician recommendation. We verify your specific benefits at absolutely no cost to you. Contact us and we will check your coverage and walk you through your options — no confusion, no surprises.

We come to you. Unlike center-based providers that require families to travel, Harrison Bridge ABA delivers every service in your child's natural environments — home, school, daycare, and community. Skills are learned where they matter most and generalize faster. We are also deeply committed to family partnership — parents are trained and involved in every step.

Every session is tailored to your child's individualized treatment plan. Sessions involve structured activities, naturalistic teaching moments, data collection, and positive reinforcement. Our therapists make sessions engaging and enjoyable. Parent involvement is encouraged — we want caregivers to observe and participate so strategies carry over throughout the day.

After your initial consultation, we verify your insurance benefits typically within a few business days. Then we schedule the BCBA assessment in your home. Once complete, we develop your child's treatment plan and therapy begins. The full process from first contact to therapy starting typically takes 2–4 weeks depending on insurance authorization timelines.

Yes. While ABA therapy is most commonly associated with Autism Spectrum Disorder, it is effective for children with developmental delays, intellectual disabilities, ADHD, and other behavioral challenges. Contact us to discuss your child's specific situation.

A BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst) is a licensed clinician who has completed advanced graduate-level education, supervised clinical experience, and passed a national certification exam in behavior analysis. At Harrison Bridge ABA, every treatment plan is designed and supervised by a BCBA.
